# Decision Filters

Apply all 4 in order. If the first one fails, verdict is a direct NO — no need to continue.

---

## Filter 1: Real Value

What specific problem does it solve that isn't already solved?

- Is there already something in the inventory that covers this need?
- If it's technology: what concrete limitation does the current equipment have?
- If it's a "nice to have": how often would you have used it in the last 6 months?
- Does it improve life in a measurable way or is it purchasing dopamine?

---

## Filter 2: Cost per Use

**Price ÷ realistic expected uses** (not the imagined ones, the actual ones)

Be skeptical with optimistic projections.

---

## Filter 3: The X $/hour Rule

**Price ÷ [your rate] = hours of work**

Calculate your rate:
```
Rate = Monthly discretionary budget ÷ Effective hours worked per month
Effective hours ≈ (52×5 days - holidays - vacations) × 8h / 12 ≈ 149 h/month
```

Example: $1,000/month ÷ 149h ≈ 6.7 → round to **$7/hour**

Update your rate here: **[X] $/hour**

Always convert to human perspective: "it's X days of work", "it's what you earn in X hours".

---

## Filter 4: BIFL Philosophy

Is it the definitive version or will there be an upgrade in 1-2 years?

- If there is a clearly superior version for 30-50% more → wait and buy that one
- If obsolescence cycle < 3 years → evaluate cost per year of use instead
- If it's something "for life" (tools, cookware, technical clothing) → go for the definitive one

---

## Verdict

**✅ YES** — Passes all 4 filters. Enjoy it without guilt.

**❌ NO** — Fails on some filter. That money looks better elsewhere.

**⏳ WAIT** — The product makes sense but the timing doesn't (new gen imminent, inflated price, upcoming sale). Indicate when the best time would be.

---

## Guidelines by Category

> Customize this section with the categories relevant to you.

**Tech**: Main equipment only with a failure or severe limitation. Accessories with strict cost/use rule.

**Subscriptions**: Calculate annual cost (monthly × 12) in hours of work. Does it justify paying forever?

**Gaming**: Verify backlog before approving. Cost/hour of gameplay: if > $1/hour, point it out.

**Trips/Experiences**: Experiences > objects. The fixed monthly savings/investment is UNTOUCHABLE.

**Kitchen**: Only if it opens up new techniques or replaces something deteriorated.
