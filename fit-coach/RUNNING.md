- If Achilles, knee, or plantar pain appears → freeze volume progression immediately

## Cadence Reference Guide

**What actually matters:** the foot should land under your center of mass, not in front. Overstriding (long stride with foot landing ahead) increases impact and injury risk. Cadence is just a proxy for that.

**Expected ranges by pace (adjust as your fitness improves):**

| Pace | Expected Cadence | Status |
|------|-----------------|--------|
| [slow Z2 pace] | [~148-155 spm] | Normal easy Z2 |
| [medium Z2 pace] | [~153-160 spm] | Normal Z2 high |
| [Z3 pace] | [~158-165 spm] | Normal Z3 |

> Cadence will increase naturally as pace improves. Don't chase arbitrary numbers.
> The commonly cited 170-180 spm applies to runners at 5:00-6:00/km pace, not beginners.

**Quick check each session:** does the foot land under the body or in front? If under → good. If you feel like you're "braking" with each stride, shorten the stride slightly and let cadence rise naturally.
